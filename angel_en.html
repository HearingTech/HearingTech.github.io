MEXICO CITY

The Independence Monument, better known as the Angel of Independence is one of the most representative symbols of Mexico City, and was inaugurated in 1910 by the then president of Mexico, Porfirio Díaz, to commemorate the Centennial of the Independence of Mexico.

Description

The height of the monument is 90.16m, plus 1.5m for the stands built in 1910 and an additional 3m for the stands built in 1986, for a total of 94.66m and showing the collapse of the city.

Pedestal

In the eastern part of the pedestal, in its upper part, there is a group of sculptures called "Apotheosis of the Father of the Nation" made up of three figures, two of them symbolic figures, standing, raising a Mexican flag, the priest Miguel Hidalgo, to the south Seated with a book and a pen, the Muse of History, to the north in an attitude of getting up and offering Hidalgo a laurel wreath is La Patria.
